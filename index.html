FUT

Follicular Unit Transplantation

Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT) is a traditional hair transplant method in which a strip of skin containing hair follicles is surgically removed from the donor area, typically the back or sides of the scalp. The strip is then dissected under a microscope into individual follicular units, which are groups of 1 to 4 hairs. These follicular units are carefully transplanted into the thinning or bald areas of the scalp.

The key benefit of FUT is that it allows for the transplantation of a larger number of hair follicles in a single session, making it suitable for patients with extensive hair loss. However, it does leave a linear scar in the donor area, which can be noticeable if the hair is cut very short. The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, and patients typically experience a longer recovery period compared to FUE, with some swelling and discomfort. Despite the scarring, FUT remains a popular choice for patients seeking a more affordable solution with a higher graft yield in one session.

FUE

Follicular Unit Extraction

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) is a modern hair transplant technique that involves extracting individual hair follicles from a donor area, typically the back or sides of the scalp, and transplanting them into areas of thinning or baldness. Unlike older methods such as Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT), which involves removing a strip of skin, FUE is less invasive and leaves minimal scarring, with small, dot-like scars that are usually not noticeable. The process is performed under local anesthesia, and the harvested follicles are carefully placed to mimic the natural growth pattern of the scalp. FUE is particularly beneficial for patients seeking a natural hairline, as the placement of grafts can be customized to match the existing hair growth. This technique also allows for quicker recovery, with most patients resuming normal activities within a few days. FUE is widely considered an effective and efficient solution for both men and women looking to restore hair density and improve their appearance.

PRP

Platelet-Rich Plasma

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is an advanced, non-surgical treatment that promotes hair growth by utilizing the body’s natural healing properties. The procedure involves drawing a small amount of the patient’s blood, processing it in a centrifuge to concentrate platelets rich in growth factors, and injecting the platelet-rich plasma into the scalp. These growth factors stimulate hair follicles, improve blood flow, and encourage the production of new hair. PRP is commonly used alongside hair transplant procedures to enhance graft survival, accelerate healing, and boost overall hair density. The treatment is minimally invasive, with little to no downtime, and is considered safe as it uses the patient’s own biological material, reducing the risk of allergic reactions or complications. Regular sessions of PRP therapy can significantly reduce hair loss, improve scalp health, and restore hair thickness, making it a popular choice for individuals seeking a natural solution for hair rejuvenation.
